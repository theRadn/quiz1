Tourist Destinations in My Hometown

Simpang Lima Gumul

Simpang Lima Gumul (SLG) is Kediri's most iconic landmark, often compared to the Arc de Triomphe in Paris due to its grand architecture. Surrounded by dancing fountains, wide gardens, and unique sculptures, it's a favorite spot for both locals and tourists to take photos or relax. Visitors can enjoy Kediri's local street food, take evening strolls under city lights, or play in nearby children's areas. The location of SLG is Simpang Lima Gumul Roundabout, Tugurejo, Ngasem District, Kediri, East Java.

Kediri Eco Park

Kediri Eco Park is a family-friendly nature and recreation park that blends environmental education with outdoor fun. Surrounded by greenery, the park offers activities like fishing, gazebos, and nature walks. It's designed to promote eco-awareness, with areas dedicated to plant nurseries, waste management education, and animal care. The park is ideal for school trips, family outings, or simply relaxing in nature. There's also a cafe and small food stalls for visitors. The location of Kediri Eco Park is in Jl. Kelapa, Ketami, Pesantren District, Kediri City, East Java.

Brantas Park

Brantas Park is a riverside green space perfect for relaxation and recreation. Set along the Brantas River, the park offers bike paths, swimming pools, playgrounds, and scenic picnic spots under large shady trees. You'll also find small food stalls serving Kediri's local dishes and even a museum nearby showcasing local culture. The location of Brantas Park is in Pocanan, Kediri City Sub-District, Kediri City, East Java.

Selomangleng Cave

Selomangleng Cave offers a blend of nature and legend. Inside the dark and humid cave, visitors can explore winding paths lined with stalactites and stalagmites. The cave is also linked to the local legend of a prince in meditation. With its natural lighting and quiet atmosphere, it's perfect for photos and peaceful reflection. This cave is located in Jl. Selomangleng, Pojok Village, Mojoroto District, Kediri City, East Java.

Airlangga Museum

Airlangga Museum is a cultural and historical museum in Kediri that showcases artifacts from the Kediri and Majapahit kingdoms. Inside, you'll find ancient statues, inscriptions, and relics that tell the story of Java's Hindu-Buddhist past. A standout piece is the sacred water jar believed to be used for rituals. It is located near Selomangleng Cave in Jl. Lingkar Maskumambang, Pojok Village, Mojoroto District, Kediri City, East Java.

Kampung Inggris (English Village)

Kampung Inggris in Pare, Kediri, is a unique village known for its intensive English learning programs. Students from across Indonesia and even abroad come here for short courses where English is used in daily life. Even if you're not joining a course, it's still an interesting place to visit and observe the educational environment. Kampung Inggris is located in Tulungrejo and Pelem Villages, Pare District, Kediri, East Java.
